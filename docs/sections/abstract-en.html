Abstract

The CEMDHRO ontology provides a semantic framework for representing high-risk medical devices within the European regulatory ecosystem. It models key entities such as devices, manufacturers, risk classifications, incidents, and corrective actions, capturing their relationships in a structured and interoperable manner. By integrating regulatory concepts from MDR and IVDR, the ontology enables enhanced traceability, risk analysis, and knowledge sharing across stakeholders. Additionally, it supports reasoning capabilities through defined class restrictions and inference rules, facilitating the identification of critical incidents and pending corrective actions. CEMDHRO aims to improve data consistency, support decision-making processes, and contribute to safer management of medical devices in clinical and regulatory contexts.
